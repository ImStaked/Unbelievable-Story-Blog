## Intro
When I was younger I was forced to participate in over 3 years of Cognitive Behaviour Therapy on both an outpatient and inpatient basis. It got boring and when I was doing my second inpatient stay I stole the counslers manual and studied it for almost a whole month. Nobody noticed it was missing and I kept it under my bed. I read it in my spare time because there was nothing else availabe to read. When I finished the Cognitive Behaviour Therapy manual I put it back where I stole it from and nobody ever said a word about it. The basic premise of CBT is that beliefs influence behaviour. If you can change a persons belief system you can alter the way they behave. The reason all of the mind control stuff I have been subjected to does not work is because I have a solid understanding of what I believe and it is based in reality that can be proven. If you believe in a bunch of fake stuff it is easy to be manipulated.

When cameras were first introduced in the 1830s and 1840s, many people truly viewed them as a magical or "sorcerous" invention. The ability to instantly capture a realistic image using light and chemicals seemed supernatural to a public unfamiliar with photographic technology. The technology used for mind control and coercive control is no different. "Any sufficiently advanced technology is indistinguishable from magic" is Arthur C. Clarke's Third Law, a famous maxim highlighting that technologies exceeding a person's understanding appear magical rather than scientific. Proposed in the 1960s-70s, this concept suggests that the boundaries of potential are constantly expanding and that advanced scientific, or extraterrestrial, capabilities often mimic supernatural acts
